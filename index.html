About Me

Since August 2025, I have been an Assistant Professor of Mathematics at Texas A&M University, focusing on numerical methods for fluid dynamics and high-performance computing. In April 2026, I will join the University of M√ºnster, where I will lead the Geophysical Fluid Dynamics research group.

I was previously a DFG Walter Benjamin Fellow at the University of California, Berkeley. My research interests include fluid mechanics, turbulence, statistical mechanics, and nonlinear or stochastic dynamics. My work uses numerical simulations, modeling, and theory to study geophysical (oceanic and atmospheric) turbulence, with a focus on self-organisation and the formation of coherent structures such as hurricane-like large-scale vortices.

At UC Berkeley, I worked in the group of Professor Edgar Knobloch and was co-supervised by Keith Julien (CU Boulder) until his untimely passing. I study instability-driven turbulence and rotating convection.
